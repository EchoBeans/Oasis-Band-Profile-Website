History of Oasis

Oasis is an English rock band formed in Manchester in 1991. The band was originally formed by Liam Gallagher, Paul Arthurs, Paul McGuigan, and Tony McCarroll. Later, Liam's older brother Noel Gallagher joined the band as the lead guitarist and main songwriter.

Oasis became one of the most successful bands of the Britpop era in the 1990s. Their debut album, Definitely Maybe (1994), became the fastest-selling debut album in British music history.

Their second album, (What's the Story) Morning Glory? (1995), featured some of their biggest hits including Wonderwall and Don't Look Back in Anger. These songs helped Oasis achieve worldwide fame.
